▶ Abstract


This paper presents a comprehensive survey and comparative analysis of recent advances in automated vehicle detection and counting systems for intelligent transportation infrastructure. As urban traffic congestion intensifies globally, accurate real-time vehicle monitoring has become essential for traffic management, urban planning, and infrastructure optimization. We review and evaluate state-of-the-art approaches spanning traditional computer vision methods, deep learning-based detectors, and hybrid architectures designed for diverse traffic scenarios.


Our analysis covers detection pipelines operating across multiple modalities including RGB cameras, infrared sensors, and LiDAR systems, with particular focus on challenges such as occlusion handling, varying illumination conditions, multi-scale vehicle representation, and real-time processing constraints. We benchmark leading architectures on standard traffic datasets and present quantitative comparisons of accuracy, throughput, and robustness under adverse weather and lighting conditions.


The study identifies key research gaps and proposes future directions including edge-optimized counting models, cross-domain transfer learning for diverse road environments, and privacy- preserving detection frameworks. Our findings offer a practical roadmap for researchers and engineers deploying vehicle counting systems in smart city infrastructure, highlighting the trade-offs between computational cost, detection accuracy, and deployment scalability.

▶ Methodology


We conducted a systematic literature review following established survey methodologies, analyzing over 100 recent publications on vehicle detection and counting. Each approach was categorized by detection paradigm (anchor-based, anchor-free, transformer-based), sensor modality, and target deployment environment. Quantitative comparisons were performed using standardized metrics on public benchmarks including UA-DETRAC and KITTI.


We further validated selected top-performing models on custom traffic footage collected from urban intersections and highway corridors under varying conditions. This real-world evaluation assessed generalization capability beyond curated benchmarks, measuring counting accuracy, false positive rates, and processing latency on both GPU servers and edge deployment hardware to provide practical deployment insights.
